POWER PLANT WATER SYSTEM

Overview and Key Components

Water systems are essential for power plant operation, serving three critical functions: cooling, steam generation, and water treatment. Key components include cooling towers (heat dissipation), boilers (steam production), condensers (steam-to-water conversion), and treatment units (water purification). Maintaining water quality is vital for system efficiency, preventing scale buildup, corrosion, and ensuring optimal heat transfer throughout the plant.

POWER PLANT WATER SYSTEM – PROCESS FLOW

Water Cycle Through the System


Raw Water Intake → Treatment → Boiler Feed → Steam Generation → Condensation → Cooling → Recycle This continuous cycle ensures efficient power generation while minimizing water waste through systematic treatment and recycling processes.
