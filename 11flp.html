WHAT IS FLUID POWER?

Understanding the Fundamentals

Fluid power is the use of fluids under pressure to generate, control, and transmit power. This technology encompasses two primary systems: hydraulic systems, which use liquids such as oil, and pneumatic systems, which use compressed air or gas. Both systems convert fluid energy into mechanical motion to perform work efficiently and precisely.

KEY COMPONENTS

Core Elements of Fluid Power Systems


Pump or Compressor: Generates fluid flow and pressure to power the system. Actuator: Converts fluid energy into mechanical motion (cylinders, motors). Control Valves: Regulate flow direction, pressure, and volume. Reservoir: Stores and conditions the working fluid.

ADVANTAGES & APPLICATIONS

Benefits and Real-World Uses


Advantages:
• High power density
• Precise control
• Flexibility in motion and force

Applications:
• Industrial machinery
• Mobile equipment (construction, agriculture)
• Aerospace systems
