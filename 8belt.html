PUMP FUNDAMENTALS

Overview of Pumps and Fluid Movement

A pump is a mechanical device that moves liquids by converting mechanical energy into hydraulic energy. The two main types are centrifugal pumps, which use rotational motion to create flow, and positive displacement pumps, which trap and move fixed volumes of fluid. Pumps are essential across industries including water supply systems, industrial manufacturing processes, and power plant operations where they circulate cooling water and feed boilers.

PUMP PERFORMANCE METRICS

Key Parameters Defining Pump Operation


Flow Rate (m³/h): Volume of fluid moved per unit time, critical for system sizing. Head (Pressure): The height a pump can lift fluid, measured in meters or pressure units. Efficiency: Ratio of hydraulic power output to mechanical power input. Power Consumption: Energy required to operate, directly linked to efficiency. Key Stat: Typical pump efficiency ranges between 60-85%.
