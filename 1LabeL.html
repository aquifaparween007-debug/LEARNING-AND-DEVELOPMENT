LUBRICATION

Reducing Friction and Wear

Lubrication is the application of a substance between moving surfaces to reduce friction and minimize wear. Key lubricant types include oils for high-speed applications and greases for sealed or s;ow-moving components. Proper lubrication is essential for equipment longevity, optimal performance, and preventing costly mechanical failures.

LUBRICATION BEST PRACTICES

Guidelines for Effective Lubrication


Proper Lubricant Selection: Choose lubricants based on operating temperature, load, speed, and environmental conditions. Match viscosity grades to manufacturer specifications.
Lubrication Intervals: Establish regular schedules based on equipment requirements. Monitor lubricant condition through sampling and visual inspection.
Avoiding Common Mistakes: Over-lubrication causes seal damage and energy waste. Under-lubrication leads to premature wear and failure. Follow precise quantity guidelines.
