BEARING ASSEMBLY

Overview of the Assembly Process

Bearings are critical components that support rotating shafts and reduce friction. Common types include ball bearings, roller bearings, and plain bearings, each suited for specific load and speed requirements. The assembly process follows three essential steps: thorough cleaning of all components, careful inspection for defects or damage, and precise fitting to ensure proper seating. Precision and cleanliness are paramount—even microscopic contaminants can cause premature bearing failure and costly equipment downtime.

BEARING ASSEMBLY CONSIDERATIONS

Key Factors for Success


Avoiding Contamination Maintain a clean workspace and use lint-free materials. Keep bearings sealed until installation and handle with clean, dry hands or gloves. Correct Mounting Techniques Apply force only to the ring being fitted. Use press fit methods for interference fits, or heat bearings uniformly (max 120°C) for thermal expansion mounting. Checking Clearances and Lubrication Verify internal clearances match specifications after mounting. Apply the correct type and quantity of lubricant before final assembly.
